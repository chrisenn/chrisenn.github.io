Viva Engage Copilot

Piloting a new AI feature

Context

Copilot in Viva Engage had been released to a subset of users. The functionality we were looking at included:

  • Get ideas for a post
  • Draft post
  • Get feedback on post
  • Rewrite post
The purpose of this research project was to get some early qualitative feedback and identify how people are using it (or why they're not) as well as any issues.

Role

  • End-to-end research (in collaboration with BA and another researcher)
  • DIY recruitment
  • Evangelization of results in multiple forums

Preparation

  • Developed research plan with feature team
  • Worked with another researcher to come up with research objectives
  • Spoke to data science colleague to ask what analytics were available and if he had observed any patterns he thought were interesting (for additional hypotheses)
  • Aligned with internal AI benchmarking team to do qualitatively what they would be doing quanitatively
  • DIY recruitment including standard tools (e.g. Respondent.io) as well as reaching out to our client-facing sales team/account managers and reaching out to our internal recruitment networks.

Execution

  • Conducted a round of semi-structured interviews with early adopters (and observers on the call)
  • There was a range of usage levels
  • Our original plan systematically went through each feature and rated them on multiple factors
  • In practice, this was not feasible within the time
  • I pivoted and eliminated redundancy by combining some sections, making the ratings less systematic and asking them in a more succinct/conversational way

Key Findings/Themes

  • Users were satisfied by time/effort saving on grunt work and specificity of content
  • Users/organizations are still transitioning which proved to be a barrier to Copilot use
  • Usability could be improved with more specific guidance and discoverability

Outcome

  • Identified use cases where it's more/less useful
  • Features did the job but there were some improvement areas identified in terms of change management and usability
  • Validated some of our in progress work
  • Presented findings to a number of different audiences including product team, UXR team, senior leadership and technical team
  • Informed design work to improve the onboarding experience, increasing discoverability and providing more guidance as to how and when to use Copilot
  • Further research (round 2 of this study as well as validation of the new design concepts, identifying ESL as an additional use case to dig into/recruit for)
